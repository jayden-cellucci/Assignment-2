George Boole

Born: Lincoln, United Kingdom Nov 2, 1815:
Died: Ballintemple, Cork, Ireland Dec 8, 1864 (aged 49)

George Boole was an English mathematician philosopher and logician.

He is known for establishing symolic algebra and algebra of logic (now called Boolean Logic) which is the basic desgin to computer circuts.

Early Life

Boole was born in Lincoln Enlgand on November 2, 1815. He learned mathematics threw his father but was mostly self-taught on-ward.

By 16, he was teaching in village schools in West Yorkshire and opened his own school by age 20.


The Law of Thoughts (1854) and Boolean Logic

In 1849 Boole was appointed the first professeor of mathematics at Queen's College in Cork Ireland. In 1854, he publsihed "An Investigation into the Law of Thoughts."

The book was only 86 pages long but was straightforward with Boole using alegbra to represent logic, this became known as "Boolean Logic".

Boole talked about the analogy between algebraic symbols and those that can represent logical forms and syllogisms, showing how the symbols of quantity can be separated from those of operation.

Boole was also one of the first Englishmen to write on logic.


Death

Boole contracted pneumonia on November 24, 1864 and died two weeks later on Dec 8, he was 49.

It is not of the essence of mathematics to be conversant with the ideas of number and quantity. Whether as a general habit of mind it would be desirable to apply symbolic processes to moral argument, is another question.


-George Boole
